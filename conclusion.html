Generating Gender Roles in Kathak: How Female Kathak Dancers See and Experience Gender

Significance and Conclusion

Dance is not a monolith, and Kathak dance even less so, if one studies its substantial transformations which have adapted to historical and societal shifts over the centuries. In light of these transformations, one cannot allow Kathak to remain stagnant in the twenty-first century. It must advance beyond the walls of decades-old texts to fit modern artists and audiences whose mindsets around gender have changed due to their drastically evolving society. While the project does not aim to preach a new gender ideology in Kathak, it does aim to draw attention to the weaknesses and reticence in its existing construction of gender.

Without researching the Kathak texts and performances that current teachers and dancers use today, there will be no progress, and nor would any meaningful changes be made without including and appreciating the opinions and experiences of the newest generations of dancers, who often rely on theoretical texts and the teachings of influential figures, including their own gurus.
